Dragon Ball/Z/GT Questions

Q) Who made Dragonball?
A) A man named Akira Toriyama, one of Japan's most talented manga authors/illustrators..

Q) When did Dragonball come out?
A) In Japan in 1984.

Q) Wait. You keep mentioning "manga" and "anime." What's the difference?
A) Manga is Japanese comics. Dragonball began as a manga. An anime is a Japanese-made cartoon, usually having a sci-fi/fantasy theme. Dragonball was made into an anime after it became popular. At what corresponds to approximetley Volume 17 of the manga, the anime began using the name "Dragonball Z," to symbolize the show's change of focus from "getting the dragonballs" to fighting. In DBZ, Goku had become an adult, and he learns he is not a human at all, but a Saiya-jin from the planet Vegeta. This set off a chain of events which sent Goku and the rest of the "Z Fighters" to other planets, and beyond. Dragonball and Dragonball Z spawned 21 movies and TV specials. More info on this in the Movies and Specials section. In 1996 Toei, the company producing DB/Z, decided to create a brand-new series, without the help of Akira Toriyama, except as a character consultent. Goku had become a child once more, as the result of a botched wish made by Emporer Pilaf, using the "Dark Star" Dragonballs, a set created by Piccolo before he seperated into two beings. It was named Dragonball GT, and it's main focus was, again, getting the dragonballs, but with a twist. The 'balls where located on other planets, and if not collected within 1 year, the Earth would explode, yadda yadda yadda. The series did poorly, even after switching to the fighting genre once more, which attracted more fans, and was cancelled in 1997. It had 1 TV special, "Goku Jr". Jeez that was a long answer...
